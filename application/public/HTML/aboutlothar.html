Who I am

I am originally from Austria, but moved to San Francisco with my family when I was eight years old. I studied mathematics as an undergrad at San Francisco State University and went on to get a PhD in mathematics at the Free University of Berlin. After a postdoctoral fellowship in Waterloo, Ontario, I decided to change career paths from mathematics to computer science and pursued a masters degree at my old alma mater. I am currently a second semester graduate student in the department of computer science at SFSU, where I am involved in research regarding the automated generation of video captions for visually impaired users.



My Experience

I taught myself programming in middle school from a "Learn to Program BASIC" cd-rom I got through a book order. I had always wanted to create games as a kid, and so through middle and high school, I worked on building role playing games using the RPG Toolkit, which came with its own scripting language called RPG Code. In college, I learned C++ from various online tutorials, and took university courses in Java and C++.



Beyond Programming

When I am not holed up in my room basking in the glow of my laptop, I can be found doing international folk dancing. I love the wide variety of beautiful music as well as the feeling of community it creates. I also enjoy travelling. Among my favorite trips were taking an RV through Alaska, and living at a Buddhist temple in Japan for six months.
